# Lanternhouse World Building

Lanternhouse takes place on Asterfen, a fog-ringed island chain where old beacon keepers, harbor traders, chapel scribes, and independent housemates keep small communities alive. The prototype focuses on Brindlewick, a warm coastal village that acts as the player's first home base.

## Core Premise

The player arrives as a practical housemate-adventurer rather than a chosen hero. The island's Lantern Line has gone dim, trade routes are unreliable, and familiar places are being cut off by fog. The playable loop is to explore, earn trust, gather supplies, relight beacons, and make the shared house safer and more useful.

## Brindlewick

Brindlewick is the first demo town: a harbor-side settlement of wood roofs, warm windows, market stalls, gardens, and small service buildings. Its people are cautious but helpful. The town supports the early demo with an inn, shops, workshop hooks, quest-giving elders, and readable NPC dialogue.

## Factions

- Keepers Guild: maintains the beacon line and old maps.
- Harbor Compact: controls docks, shipping, and hard-to-get goods.
- Grey Chapel: records local history and watches over the dead.
- The Unlit: west-side villagers who distrust official beacon doctrine.

## Prototype Story Arc

1. Arrive in Brindlewick and learn that the lighthouse wick is cold.
2. Explore the overworld, gather supplies, and find the first beacon routes.
3. Relight enough of the Lantern Line to open the sealed cave.
4. Win a first combat showcase and return to town with proof that the demo loop works.

## Demo Dialogue Direction

NPC dialogue should stay grounded and neighborly: trade gossip, old beacon lore, weather warnings, and practical advice. Avoid villain monologues or chosen-one framing. The strongest tone is classic adventure: a small place with history, danger at the edges, and people trying to keep the lights on.

## Art Direction Notes

The public demo should show a cohesive 16-bit JRPG presentation: pixel-perfect town buildings, readable 3/4 roofs/facades, symbolic overworld landmarks, fog-of-war that still lets UI labels read clearly, and side-view battle framing with grounded enemies and party sprites.
